Part B – Prevention Checklist:

Create a 'Stay Safe Online' checklist with at least 8 specific, actionable prevention

tips relevant to college students in India.

* Avoid clicking suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages, especially those asking for personal or financial details.
* Download apps only from trusted sources like the Google Play Store or Apple App Store to reduce the risk of malware.
* Be careful while using public Wi-Fi in places like cafes or colleges; avoid logging into banking or payment apps on public networks.
* Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank or a payment service. (UPI Safety Tip)
* Always verify UPI payment requests before approving them, and remember that receiving money does not require entering your UPI PIN. (UPI Safety Tip)
* Regularly check your bank and UPI transaction history to quickly identify any unauthorized transactions.
* Log out from shared computers and devices after accessing your email, social media, or financial accounts.
* Report cyber fraud immediately through the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or by calling the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
